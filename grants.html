Chelsie Wright Fund

Each year Chelsie’s mom and Friends put on a very large fundraiser in Chelsea’s name and those funds are sent too rare to help families from all over.
These funds will go to families that have the same condition as Chelsie in our get out and move program and the rare give back.
Programs like that assist special-needs families obtain special equipment or special things throughout the year and we couldn’t do without these kinds of programs.
It is an honor that Ashleigh and her friends/family have chosen to continue helping and blessing other families through Chelsie’s name.

Most important though Chelsie was a beautiful soul that we want to celebrate all the time and every day.
She’s a reminder that Schizencephaly is unpredictable and unfair and how quickly it can change everything.
We miss you so much Chelsie!


The Dexter Owen RARE Fund

Dexter a sweet happy kid that loved his dog and his momma awas born with bilateral Schizencephaly. Regardless of the seizures he endured regularly he had a smile no matter what except in physical therapy where Dexter was not a fan. Dexter fell asleep and likely passed away from SUDEP, a common underlying condition of uncontrollable seizures in refractory epilepsy. What Is Refractory Epilepsy? If your doctor says you have refractory epilepsy, it means that medicine isn’t bringing your seizures under control. You might hear the condition called by some other names, such as uncontrolled,intractable, or drug-resistant epilepsy. WebMD Dexter’sfamily has continued his legacy through a Charity Run yearly and donate funds to We are rare to distribute to families in need. Dexter as a reminder of Schizencephaly and it’s unpredictable nature, how quickly things change and how we have to embrace every day and every moment. Dexter’s smile will live on through the hearts of many children that he touches in the next coming years through his memory and help to other children with Schizencephaly.

The Ayra Foundation

Provides financial aid for the purchase of medical supplies and equipment for children with medical conditions. Applications will only be accepted from parents or legal guardians on behalf of a child who is: 18 years or younger, Request qualifies as a valid health care need, Child lives in St. Louis and the surrounding area, Referred by a social worker or case manager, Request is up to $1,000, You are allowed one piece of equipment per year. Anything over $1,000, you are responsible for the remaining balance.
www.thearyafoundation.org
